SPOTLIGHT

Book by Teams is here, and the early numbers are turning heads.

Our biggest mover, month after month

MORI continues to be the biggest mover and shaker we have, and it isn't close. This month we wrapped up Book by Teams, which a lot of you have been waiting on. In plain terms: MORI can now check availability and book into the right team for the job, so a quick lube request lands in your quick lube shop and main shop work lands in your main shop, all driven by your opcodes and transportation types. It works much the way it does in OLS, and you can turn it on yourself right from the MORI AI tab.

We also tightened up the booking logic underneath it. MORI now validates the requested day and time against your actual availability before it books (so a day you've marked full stays full), and it handles invalid opcodes gracefully instead of pushing a blank line to the DMS. Small things on paper, big things at the service desk.

A modernized browser for integrated apps

The integrated applications inside the DMS (Autovance and PowerLane among them) now run on a newer, more modern browser interface. That sounds like plumbing, and it is, but it's the kind of plumbing you feel every day: better performance, better stability, and fewer compatibility headaches. It's a foundation we'll keep building on.

Push F&I updates only (without re-pushing the whole deal)

This one is for our F&I Menu users on Autovance and Quorum DMS. The two teams built a new integration option that lets you send only the F&I-related updates from Autovance to the DMS (fees, F&I products, extended warranties, insurance products, and vehicle pricing) instead of re-pushing the entire deal.

The practical win: any changes you've made directly on the DMS worksheet stay intact, while your F&I information stays up to date. No more stepping on your own work to keep the menu in sync.

PowerLane: All-Makes maintenance schedules, powered by Motor Data

Colten and the PowerLane team took a big step this month. We've brought Motor Data into PowerLane, which puts factory maintenance schedules for all makes right in the lane. So whatever rolls into your drive, your advisors can see the manufacturer-recommended services for that vehicle (with estimated labor pricing right on each item) and sell maintenance with confidence, without flipping between systems or guessing. It's available as an optional add-on, so if it's something you'd want for your shop, reach out and we'll walk you through it.

We also resolved a longstanding issue with the Work Order tech copy printing, which I know has been a thorn for some of you for a while. That's now sorted.

And coming next is something I'm genuinely excited about for the lane: auto-retry and offline detection. If your shop has spots where the wifi gets sketchy, PowerLane will handle it far more gracefully, so your advisors keep moving instead of fighting the connection.

New: Expense Variance Report in QAnalytics

This one came straight from conversations with you. The feedback we kept hearing was "it would be great to see my expense variance year over year, or month over month." So we built it. The new Expense Variance Report gives you a fresh look at expenses with the ability to compare against a prior period of your choosing.

You set the comparison (yearly, quarterly, or by period), pick your prior and current periods, and filter to a single department if you'd rather. From there you can look at variance by department in summary or detail, or drop down to the account level and expand a single account to see exactly which departments are driving the change.

Early days on two more we're excited about

First, Parts Price Codes. Today, when your counter or service lane needs to apply a one-off discount that isn't tied to a specific associate, staff end up working out the discounted price on a calculator and typing it into the Price Used field. Price Codes fix that. You'll be able to set up your own discount rules (think Retail less a percentage, or Cost plus a percentage), pick one right on a part sale or work order, and have the DMS calculate the price for you. It applies just to that transaction, and the code shows on the line so it's easy to see what was applied and why. Watch for it in the next couple of releases.

Second, we've started discovery and design on an accounting P&L forecasting tool, built to help you project your profit and loss and plan ahead with real numbers from the DMS. We're working through it with a dealer partner right now, and I'd love a few more voices in that conversation. If P&L forecasting is something you'd want a say in, raise your hand and we'll bring you in.

BDC Lead Performance, now on the Dashboard

We added a new BDC Lead Performance section to the Dashboard, with two views that answer two different questions. The BDC Lead Funnel follows a single group of leads through every stage (Processed, Engaged, Appointments, Shows, Sold), so your conversion rates stay consistent and meaningful. The BDC Activity Performance view summarizes raw output in a timeframe (leads assigned, processed, appointments created, shows, and sales). One tells you how well leads convert, the other tells you how much work the team is putting out, and together they give managers a real read on the BDC.

Google Address Lookup for new customers

Address Lookup (powered by Google Places) is now in the desktop web app too. It suggests and auto-fills addresses as you type, matching what the Sales Mobile App has had all along.

Sales Mobile App

Steady investment in mobile, starting with notifications

We've been putting real, ongoing work into the Sales Mobile App. The headline this round is push notifications, which now arrive more consistently and reliably (nothing for you to do on your end). Alongside that, adding a customer to the Traffic Log and setting a status now auto-checks the matching Steps to the Sale (for stores with steps automated from traffic status), and that behavior is finally consistent across the app and the desktop, so there's less manual cleanup after the fact.

This is just the start. We're going to keep investing in the mobile app between now and the fall, with more functionality on the way.

Service CRM

More MORI, plus a Gmail win

Your inventory, in front of millions: the CarBuzz integration

This is one of the first things I'm excited to show off from the Valsoft side of the house. The Autovance team has been working with CarBuzz to build an integration into their new marketplace product. CarBuzz is part of Valnet, a Valsoft sister company, and their ecosystem (CarBuzz.com, TopSpeed.com, and HotCars.com) sees roughly 20 million page views a month.

With this connection in place, your active inventory can be surfaced to car shoppers reading relevant content (think a vehicle featured in an article paired with live local listings). It's a new, high-quality top-of-funnel channel for your store, and it's exactly the kind of thing being part of the Valsoft family opens up. More to come as we expand it.

More control over your team, without calling support

We rolled out a series of User Management improvements in Desk Settings. Any user with Desk Settings access can now see the full list of users at the dealership, and edit salesperson and user profiles directly (names, email, title, provincial registration, default managers, and deal role dropdowns) without going through Admin or support. We also cleaned up the table to match the rest of Desk Settings and moved the delete button inside the edit modal so nobody removes a salesperson by accident.

GM dealers: GM Protection is fully integrated in the F&I Menu

If you're a GM dealer, this one's for you. We now have GM Protection fully integrated into the Autovance F&I Menu, from rating to registration right through to electronic signature on the service contract. It's the kind of end-to-end integration that actually saves time at the desk. If you'd like it turned on for your store, let us know and we'll get it added.

A rebuilt Notes & Activities section

The team overhauled the Notes & Activities section, and I think it brings real value day to day. You can now filter with quick-select chips (User Notes, Deal Views, Documents, or Activity) instead of scrolling through everything, filters update in real time, and multi-select lets you combine categories.

We also fixed note entry so you can write multi-line notes right in the input field (Enter for a new line, Ctrl+Enter or the Note button to post). One thing to keep in mind: multi-line notes live in Autovance and won't carry over to your DMS.

Tighter sync with Quorum DMS

A couple of changes make the Autovance-to-DMS handoff cleaner. Speculative vehicles pushed to Quorum DMS now default to dealership ownership (and no longer require a customer on the deal to complete the push), and the Stock # you enter in Autovance now flows through to the Unit # field in the DMS so your inventory stays easy to cross-reference.
